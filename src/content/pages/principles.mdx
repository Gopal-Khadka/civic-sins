We are not here to lecture you. We are here to catch you doing the small public
thing you swear you never do, then make it funny enough that you send it on.
Telling someone off in the street starts a fight. A comic says the same thing
and gets a laugh instead of an argument.

## Five rules

1. **Aim at the behaviour.** The joke is the habit, never the person, their job,
   or where they come from.
2. **Make it specific.** "Turns the reel volume up when you glance over" beats
   "loud people" every time.
3. **Let the hypocrite get caught.** The loudest complainer is usually the
   offender. The reader should spot it before the character does.
4. **Punch across, never down.** Bad habits cut across every class and income.
   So do our targets. When in doubt, the offender is us.
5. **Start with ourselves.** Dey owns his own sins on the record. We point
   inward before we ever point out.

## What this is not

Not political: no parties, no leaders, no sides. Not aimed at any group: the
target is always a behaviour, never a nationality, class, or community. And not
a lecture: no moralising, no rules-of-conduct page pretending to be a comic.
