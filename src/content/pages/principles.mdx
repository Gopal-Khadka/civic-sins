This project teaches civic sense through comics that are funny, memorable, and
deliberately *uncomfortable* — designed to make you recognise yourself in the offender.
Direct correction starts fights; a comic is the one way of "saying something" that
lands without an argument on the street.

## The five pillars

1. **Sting through self-recognition.** The strip should make you wince at *yourself*,
   not feel superior to a stranger. Favour the last-panel turn.
2. **Specificity is the weapon.** Exact, observed behaviour ("turns the reel volume
   *up* when you glance over") beats generic complaint ("loud people") every time.
3. **Satire from inside the crowd.** The narrator is a self-aware hypocrite who gets
   caught committing the very sin he preached against.
4. **Punch across, never down.** Targets cut across class. The joke is never the vendor
   or the labourer; it's the behaviour. When in doubt, make the mascot the offender.
5. **No saints.** The creator openly owns their own civic sins. Audiences forgive an
   honest hypocrite; they never forgive a fraud.

## What this is not

It is **not political** — no parties, leaders, or partisan framing. It is **not targeted
at any group** — targets are *behaviours*, never nationalities, classes, or communities.
And it is **not a lecture** — no moralising, no rules-of-conduct pages as the point.
