Civic Sins is a comic project about civic sense: the small public habits that
make a shared space better or worse. Litter, noise, queue-jumping, the shopping
cart left rolling across a car park. The kind of thing everyone complains about
and most of us do anyway.

The trick is where the joke points. We do not laugh at the stranger across the
street. We catch the reader, and usually ourselves. Dey, the narrator, is a
self-aware hypocrite who condemns a habit in one panel and commits it in the
next. If we are going to point at anyone, we point inward first.

## Why comics

Correcting someone in public starts a fight. A comic says the same thing and
gets a laugh instead of an argument. It is easier to drop a habit you can laugh
at than one you are being lectured about.

## What it is not

Not political: no parties, no leaders, no sides. Not aimed at any group: the
target is always a behaviour, never a nationality, class, or community. And not
for profit. This is a hobby project, not a business.
